Course Description

CA Foundation is the first level of the Chartered Accountancy course. Students are eligible for CA Foundation after passing the Intermediate exam (after 10th). After successfully clearing the CA Foundation level, students become eligible to proceed to the next stage of the CA course, i.e., CA Intermediate.

Eligibility

Students are eligible for CA Foundation after passing Intermediate exam (after 10th).

Passing Criteria

To pass the CA Foundation exam, a student must score:

  • Minimum 50% marks in aggregate, i.e., 200 out of 400.
  • At least 40% marks in each paper, i.e., 40 out of 100.

Exam Pattern

  • Accounts – Descriptive
  • Law – Descriptive
  • Quantitative Aptitude – Objective (0.25 marks are deducted for every wrong answer)
  • Economics – Objective (0.25 marks are deducted for every wrong answer)

CA Foundation – Subjects (4 Papers, 100 Marks each)

Paper 1 – Accounting – 100 M
Paper 2 – Business Laws – 100 M
Paper 3 – Quantitative Aptitude – 100 M
Paper 4 – Business Economics – 100 M
